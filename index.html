1963

The Founding of McLaren

In 1963, New Zealand-born race car driver Bruce McLaren founded the McLaren Racing Team. The team made its debut in the Formula One World Championship at the 1966 Monaco Grand Prix.

This marked the beginning of McLaren's journey in the world of motorsports, laying the foundation for a brand synonymous with innovation and racing excellence.

1968

First Formula One Grand Prix Victory

McLaren secured its first Formula One Grand Prix victory at the 1968 Belgian Grand Prix. Driven by Bruce McLaren himself, the M7A car powered by a Ford Cosworth DFV engine crossed the finish line first, establishing McLaren as a formidable competitor in Formula One.

1992

Introduction of the McLaren F1 Supercar

In 1992, McLaren unveiled the McLaren F1, a revolutionary supercar that set new benchmarks in automotive design and performance.

With its carbon-fiber monocoque chassis, central driving position, and a BMW-sourced V12 engine, the F1 became the fastest production car of its time, achieving a top speed of 240.1 mph (386.4 km/h). The McLaren F1 remains an iconic symbol of engineering prowess and innovation.

1995

Partnership with Mercedes-Benz

In 1995, McLaren entered into a significant partnership with Mercedes-Benz, which led to the formation of the McLaren Mercedes Formula One team.

This collaboration resulted in a period of success, including winning the Drivers' Championship in 1998 and 1999 with Mika Häkkinen and the Constructors' Championship in 1998. The partnership also led to the development of the Mercedes-Benz SLR McLaren, a high-performance grand tourer.

2010

Launch of the McLaren Automotive Division

In 2010, McLaren expanded its operations beyond motorsports by launching McLaren Automotive, a division dedicated to producing high-performance road cars.

The first model, the McLaren MP4-12C, was introduced in 2011, featuring cutting-edge technology and design elements derived from McLaren's Formula One expertise. This marked McLaren's foray into the commercial automotive market, establishing it as a leading manufacturer of luxury sports cars.
